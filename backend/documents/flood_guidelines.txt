NDMA FLOOD EVACUATION AND SAFETY MANUAL

GENERAL FLOOD PROTOCOLS
Flooding is one of the most common natural disasters in India. The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) outlines clear guidelines for families caught in high-risk zones. If floodwaters begin to rise inside your region, immediately disable all central gas lines and shut down the primary household electrical circuit breaker board. Do not touch electrical equipment while standing in water or if your hands are wet to prevent severe shock.

EVACUATION PROCEDURES
When municipal bodies issue an active evacuation directive:
1. Immediately pack an emergency survival bag containing drinking water, dry rations, dry clothes, a flashlight, critical medical prescriptions, and a battery-powered radio.
2. Evacuate your residence immediately. Do not linger to pack household valuables.
3. Move only along pre-approved governmental evacuation bypass routes.
4. Avoid taking shortcuts through flooded alleys or low-lying agricultural zones, as currents can be deceptive.
5. If you are stranded, move to high grounds (schools, temples, tall concrete structures) and signal for rescue teams.

SURVIVAL IN WATER CAMPS
If caught in active floodwaters:
1. Do not walk or swim through moving water. As little as 6 inches of rapidly moving water can sweep a full-grown adult off their feet.
2. If forced to walk in standing water, utilize a long stick or rod to probe the depth and structural stability of the ground in front of you.
3. Keep away from fallen power lines, transmission grids, and suspended electric wires. Electrocution in floodwaters is a major source of localized disaster casualties.
4. Only consume boiled or filtered water. Do not drink raw river or ground floodwaters under any circumstances due to chemical and biological waste contamination. Use emergency chlorine water tablets if available.
